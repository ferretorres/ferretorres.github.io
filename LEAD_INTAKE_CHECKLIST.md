# Ferre Torres B.V. lead intake checklist

Use this to qualify inbound messages, LinkedIn conversations, warm intros, and demo requests.

## Fast triage

Score each lead from 0 to 2.

| Signal | 0 | 1 | 2 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Buyer seniority | unclear | influencer | budget owner or executive sponsor |
| Workflow clarity | vague AI interest | broad business area | specific workflow or decision loop |
| Data access | unknown | likely available | clear systems or documents |
| Business metric | none | directional | measurable result |
| Urgency | no timeline | exploratory | active pressure or target date |
| Production intent | demo curiosity | pilot interest | MVP-to-production intent |

Total:

- 0-4: nurture or ask clarifying questions
- 5-8: offer guided demo or assessment
- 9-12: propose assessment, roadmap, MVP, or architecture review

## Reply when the lead is vague

```text
Hi [Name],

The useful way to start is to identify one workflow where AI could prove value quickly.

The three things I would need to understand are:

1. Which workflow is slow, manual, expensive, or hard to scale?
2. Which data or tools does that workflow depend on?
3. What result would make an MVP worth continuing?

If helpful, this page explains the process:
https://ferretorres.eu/ai-consulting-process.html

Best,
Josep
```

```text
Hi [Name],

Yes, a guided demo makes sense.

The useful part is mapping one preview to your real workflow, data sources, users, and MVP proof point.

Before the call, it would help to know:

- which workflow you want to improve
- who uses it
- which tools or data sources are involved
- what the first MVP should prove
```

```text
- management reporting
```

## When to say no or pause

Pause if:

- no owner exists
- no workflow is defined
- no access to data or synthetic substitute is possible
- the buyer only wants a generic chatbot
- they need legal certification or regulated approval beyond the company scope
- they want named client proof that cannot be shared

Suggested wording:

```text
This may be too early for an implementation engagement.

The next useful step would be to define the workflow, owner, data sources, and success metric. Once those are clear, it becomes possible to decide whether an assessment, roadmap, or MVP makes sense.
```
