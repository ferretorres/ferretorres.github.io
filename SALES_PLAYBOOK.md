# Ferre Torres B.V. sales playbook

Use this as the operating guide for turning the site into conversations with CEOs, CFOs, CTOs, founders, and transformation leaders.

## Positioning

Short version:

Ferre Torres B.V. helps companies move from AI ambition to production systems: Company Brain, RAG, agentic workflows, finance intelligence, AI PM assistants, and AI-native internal software.

Core wedge:

Start with one high-value workflow, validate it through an MVP or PoC, then scale the AI infrastructure that proves useful.

Avoid:

- generic AI transformation language without a workflow
- named client claims without approval
- public pricing before package strategy is decided
- implying legal, regulatory, or certification status
- presenting the company as a one-person freelancer operation

## Best-fit prospects

Strong fit:

- CEO, COO, CFO, CTO, Head of AI, Head of Data, or Transformation leader owns the conversation
- company has repeated knowledge, reporting, finance, operations, support, compliance, or project-delivery friction
- data exists in documents, dashboards, tickets, CRM, ERP, email, SharePoint, Google Drive, SQL, or custom systems
- buyer wants a working MVP, not a workshop-only engagement
- there is a clear business metric or executive pressure

Weak fit:

- buyer wants "AI training" only
- no workflow owner
- no data access path
- no budget owner
- no tolerance for MVP validation
- they only want a chatbot demo with no production path

## First-call agenda

1. Buyer role and business pressure
2. Workflow or decision loop causing friction
3. Users affected and current process
4. Data sources, tools, permissions, and constraints
5. What the first MVP or PoC should prove
6. Production risks: security, hosting, evaluation, ownership, monitoring
7. Recommended route: no-build, assessment, roadmap, MVP, or architecture review

## Qualification questions

Business:

- Who owns the business result?
- What is currently slow, expensive, unreliable, or hard to scale?
- What would make leadership say the MVP worked?
- Is the goal speed, cost reduction, quality, margin insight, risk control, or new revenue?

Technical:

- Which systems hold the relevant data?
- Are permissions already defined?
- Is there a preferred cloud, stack, or hosting constraint?
- What usage volume or concurrency matters?
- What outputs need human review?

Commercial:

- Is there budget for a serious MVP or only exploration?
- Who signs off on scope?
- What timeline matters?
- What happens if the MVP proves value?
