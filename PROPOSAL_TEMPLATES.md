# Ferre Torres B.V. proposal templates

Use these as starting points for proposals after a qualified first call. Keep pricing out until the commercial strategy is decided.

## Proposal rules

- Keep the proposal tied to one workflow, one owner, and one measurable result.
- Do not use named client references unless explicitly approved.
- Do not claim certifications, legal advice, regulatory approval, or guaranteed outcomes.
- Position Ferre Torres B.V. as founder-led with a senior delivery network, not as a freelancer profile.
- Show the path from first proof to production before discussing broader transformation.
- Use the Dutch B.V. scope only.

## Route 1: AI opportunity assessment

Use when the company has many AI ideas and no clear first build.

```text
Recommended route: AI opportunity assessment

Objective:
Identify the AI workflow with the strongest overlap between business value, feasibility, data readiness, sponsor strength, and implementation speed.

Deliverables:
- AI workflow opportunity map
- Ranked shortlist of use cases
- First MVP recommendation
- Data and architecture assumptions
- Risks, blockers, and no-build candidates
- Recommended next route

Success criteria:
- Leadership can choose the first workflow to fund.
- The first MVP candidate has a clear owner, user group, data path, and measurable result.
- Weak or premature AI ideas are explicitly deprioritized.
```

Reference page: `https://ferretorres.eu/ai-opportunity-assessment.html`

## Route 2: AI integration roadmap

Use when the workflow portfolio is clear but architecture, sequencing, or governance is not.

```text
Recommended route: AI integration roadmap

Objective:
Define how AI connects to workflows, data sources, dashboards, RAG, agents, permissions, evaluation, monitoring, and production ownership.

Deliverables:
- Workflow and system map
- Integration sequence
- Company Brain / RAG / agent architecture outline
- Governance and approval points
- MVP sequence and scale gates
- Production constraints and ownership model

Success criteria:
- Business and technical teams agree on the first build sequence.
- Architecture choices are visible before implementation starts.
- The roadmap separates quick validation from production hardening.
```

Reference page: `https://ferretorres.eu/ai-integration-roadmap.html`

## Route 3: AI MVP or PoC

Use when one workflow, sponsor, and data path are ready enough to build.

```text
Recommended route: AI MVP or PoC

Objective:
Build a narrow working AI system that tests usefulness, data readiness, workflow fit, quality, governance, and production potential.

Deliverables:
- Working MVP or PoC
- Synthetic or real-data demo environment depending on access constraints
- Basic user workflow
- Evaluation and quality checks
- Security and permissions assumptions
- MVP findings and scale recommendation

Success criteria:
- Target users can evaluate a working flow.
- The system proves or disproves the intended business value.
- The company can decide whether to harden, expand, pause, or redirect.
```

Reference page: `https://ferretorres.eu/ai-mvp-poc.html`

## Route 4: Architecture review

Use when there is already an AI idea or prototype, but the production path is unclear.

```text
Recommended route: AI architecture review

Objective:
Review the technical path for RAG, agents, Company Brain, dashboards, permissions, hosting, evaluation, monitoring, and ownership.

Deliverables:
- Architecture review memo
- Risk and readiness assessment
- Data and permissions review
- Evaluation and observability recommendations
- MVP-to-production hardening path
- Recommended next build steps

Success criteria:
- CTO or technical owner can see what must change before production.
- Leadership understands technical risk and scale constraints.
- The next implementation phase has clear boundaries.
```

Reference page: `https://ferretorres.eu/ai-architecture.html`

## Route 5: Guided AI demo to MVP scope

Use when the buyer needs to visualize the system before scoping.

```text
Recommended route: Guided AI demo to MVP scope

Objective:
Use synthetic previews to align business and technical stakeholders around one real workflow and decide the right MVP path.

Deliverables:
- Guided walkthrough of relevant preview
- Workflow-to-demo mapping
- First MVP candidate
- Data, user, and governance assumptions
- Recommended next route

Success criteria:
- Buyer sees how a generic preview maps to their own workflow.
- The next step becomes concrete: assessment, roadmap, MVP, architecture review, or no-build.
```

Reference page: `https://ferretorres.eu/guided-ai-demo.html`

## Proposal quality checklist

- Does it name the workflow?
- Does it name the business metric?
- Does it name the buyer and technical owner?
- Does it separate MVP from production hardening?
- Does it include assumptions and dependencies?
- Does it avoid unapproved client names and logos?
- Does it avoid public pricing unless approved?
- Does it include a clear next decision?
