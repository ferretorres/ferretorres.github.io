Finance reporting automation that explains the numbers.

A pattern for companies where finance teams repeatedly rebuild reports, explain margin movement, chase anomalies, and answer management questions through static spreadsheets or BI queues.

Pattern summary

Move from static reporting to finance intelligence workflows.

  • Problem: reporting cycles depend on manual extraction, spreadsheet reconciliation, and repeated explanations.
  • Workflow: ingest finance data, normalize key metrics, detect movement, and generate explainable management views.
  • Data involved: ERP exports, invoices, bank data, CRM, budgets, forecasts, pricing tables, and spreadsheet models.
  • Typical MVP window: 2-5 weeks after agreeing the first report, metric definitions, and sample data.

What gets built

A finance intelligence layer that reduces repeated BI and reporting loops.

Metric model

Shared definitions for revenue, margin, cash, forecast, anomalies, pricing, and cohort views.

Dashboard surface

Interactive views that show current status, movement, drilldowns, and narrative explanations.

AI assistant

Question answering over finance context with chart explanations, variance summaries, and next-step prompts.

Controls

Source references, data freshness checks, approval notes, and clear separation between draft and final reporting.

Reusable system

The MVP becomes a reporting product, not another one-off dashboard.

  • Reusable metric definitions reduce disagreement between reports, dashboards, and management updates.
  • Anomaly and pricing signals can become automated weekly workflows with finance review.
  • The same data layer can support board packs, SaaS-style dashboards, and finance assistants.
